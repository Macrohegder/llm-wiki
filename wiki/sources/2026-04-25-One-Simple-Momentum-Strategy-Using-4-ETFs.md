One Simple Momentum Strategy Using 4 ETFs
might sound almost too basic to work, but simplicity is often where the edge hides.
While most investors chase complexity, layering indicators and overfitting models, some of the most robust strategies rely on a single, well-documented factor: momentum.
In this article, we test a straightforward rotation strategy across SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust (SPY), iShares MSCI Emerging Markets ETF (EEM), iShares 20+ Year Treasury Bond ETF (TLT), and iShares MSCI EAFE ETF (EFA), four liquid ETFs that together cover U.S. equities, international markets, emerging markets, and long-term government bonds. The idea is simple: own the asset with the strongest performance, and rotate as leadership changes.
There are no complicated signals, no macro forecasts, and no discretionary decisions. Just a rules-based approach designed to capture persistent trends across asset classes.
The question is: can something this simple actually outperform buy-and-hold? Let’s find out.
