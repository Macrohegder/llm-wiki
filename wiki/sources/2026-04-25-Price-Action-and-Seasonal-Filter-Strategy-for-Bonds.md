This post outlines a simple framework I’ve been using to approach TLT. It’s built on just two inputs:
Price action
Seasonality
No macro forecasts. No Fed-watching. Just behavior.
Price Action Trigger
Before anything else, price has to confirm.
That might sound obvious, but it’s often ignored in bond markets, where narratives tend to dominate.
In isolation, price action works, but it’s inconsistent. You get stretches where signals perform well, followed by periods where they degrade.
That’s where the second variable comes in: A seasonal filter.
The Seasonal Filter
TLT is not seasonally neutral.
Certain periods of the year and the month tend to favor strength, while others lean toward weakness. It’s about
tendencies
.
These seasonal effects are likely a reflection of institutional rebalancing flows.
