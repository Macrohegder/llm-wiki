In this post, we take a look at Keltner Channel to see what it is, how they are calculated, how they differ from Bollinger Bands, and if it’s possible to make money on Keltner Bands. We test a Keltner Channel trading strategy.
We backtest the following trading rules:
➨ The close is above the upper band. Enter at the close.
➨ Sell when the close is below the “typical price”. Exit at the close.
➨ Optimize by using a period from 2 to 50 days.
➨ We optimize by using different ATRs from 1 to 2.
The best results are clustered around 6-10 days (periods) and an ATR from 1 to 1.5. We backtest using six days and 1.3 ATR.
