Hello Readers,
Imagine hiring a security guard for your home who only works from 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM. If a fire starts at midnight, or a burglar breaks in at 3:00 AM, he’s not there. He’s “off the clock.”
This is exactly how the US Equity market functions. It is open for a mere 6.5 hours (tradeable liquidity) a day, five days a week. That leaves 17.5 hours each day plus entire weekends where your capital is locked in a burning building with no exit.
We do not live in a 9-to-5 world.
Geopolitical conflicts, currency devaluations, and banking failures do not wait for the Opening Bell.
Information travels instantly.
Thirty years ago,
news of a banking crisis
might have taken days to fully impact global sentiment. Today, a single tweet or a leaked report spreads to millions of people in seconds. The reaction function of the human herd has accelerated to light speed, yet the stock market remains stuck in a 19th-century operating window.
Wall St History: 19th Century, by John A. Rolph
When war breaks out in Europe, it’s often 2:00 AM in New York.
When an Asian bank collapses, Wall Street is asleep.
If you are holding SPY (S&P 500) blindly, you are exposed to “Gap Risk.” You go to bed with your portfolio intact and wake up to a market that has already repriced 5% lower before you can even click “Sell.”
The “Safe” Assets Are Failing You
For decades, the standard advice was simple: “Buy Bonds. Diversify.”
But as the
WisdomTree “Bitcoin Correlations” research paper
demonstrates, the rules have changed. In 2022, we saw a correlation breakdown that terrified institutional investors:
Stocks and Bonds fell together.
The correlation coefficient between the S&P 500 and U.S. Treasuries approached
positive 1.0
.
The “flight to safety” trade is broken because in a liquidity crisis, everything correlates to 1. Investors sell whatever is liquid to cover margin calls. Relying on traditional diversification is no longer a hedge, its a liability.
Testing the “Canary” Hypothesis
The theory is compelling because Bitcoin trades 24/7 and reacts instantly to global liquidity, it should signal danger before the S&P 500 opens.
Research from
Delphi Digital
supports this, suggesting Bitcoin often tops weeks before equities (warning of the 2022 crash nearly two months in advance). But theory isn’t enough. We need hard data.
So, I decided to run an experiment.
I ran a series of backtests in
RealTest
to answer one question:
Can a specific signal from another market efficiently rotate us out of the S&P 500 before a crash?
I tested the most common institutional settings to find the truth.
The results were fascinating. Most common settings failed miserably, lagging the market or getting chopped to pieces. But one specific configuration stabilized the signal,
doubling the Sharpe Ratio from 0.50 to 1.07
and significantly reducing maximum drawdown.
