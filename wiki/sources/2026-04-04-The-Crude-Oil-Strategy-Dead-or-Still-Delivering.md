Today, we examine whether a specific crude oil strategy is dead or still delivering.
We have never been big fans of trading crude oil. It is a notoriously difficult contract (ETF) to trade consistently over many years.
In our experience, systematic strategies in crude oil tend to break down faster and more often than in markets like stocks, bonds, or even gold (which is not exactly easy either).
That said, crude oil has always attracted traders. It is volatile, highly liquid, and influenced by macro trends, geopolitics, and supply shocks. But volatility alone does not create an edge. In fact, macro-driven markets can be extremely noisy and completely random.
The real question is simple: does our straightforward, rule-based crude oil strategy still work?
Crude oil strategy – trading rules
In this article, we revisit a crude oil strategy we first published on our website roughly ten years ago.
Here are the rules:
• Calculate the 25-day average of the daily High minus Low range.
• Today must be Tuesday or Thursday.
• Today’s close must be lower than yesterday’s close minus the 25-day average range.
• Sell at the close of the next trading day.

Here are the key performance metrics:

The raw numbers are not terrible, except for the 28% drawdown, but the more concerning issue is performance since 2022. The strategy has struggled in recent years.
Crude oil is EXTREMELY difficult to trade. Edges tend to deteriorate more quickly than in structurally rising markets such as equities or bonds, where long-term upward drift provides a natural tailwind.
It is also a reminder that edges rarely disappear overnight. They usually decay gradually.
We started by asking whether this crude oil strategy is dead or still delivering.
Based on recent performance, the answer appears to be no.
