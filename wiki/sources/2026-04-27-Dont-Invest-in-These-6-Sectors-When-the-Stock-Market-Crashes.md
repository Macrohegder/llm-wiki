I ran

The sectors you rotate into during a crash give you the same annual return as the best refuge assets in the test. But with two to three times the drawdown.
Holding Financials (XLF) when SPY is in crash mode:
7.5% annual return, -76% maximum drawdown.
The best-ranked asset in this test produces a similar annual return with less than a third of that drawdown.
Same return and triple the drawdown. You are not rotating to safety because you are paying three times the drawdown for nothing.
Healthcare during crashes:
-44% average drawdown.
Utilities:
-48%. The sectors that feel defensive are some of the worst assets you can hold when it matters most.
The problem is you probably don’t know exactly which ones destroy capital and which ones actually hold up.
Retail traders usually find out during the crash, not before it. That’s too late.
