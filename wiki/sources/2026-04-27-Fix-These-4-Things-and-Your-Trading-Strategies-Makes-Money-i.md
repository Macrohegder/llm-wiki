If you want your systematic trading strategies to perform better, you need to focus on fixing these four critical mistakes.
I’ve analyzed hundreds of failed backtests and live trading accounts, and it turns out that underperforming strategies almost always suffer from one or more of these issues. If you solve for these, your win rate and profitability will improve immediately.
In this article, I’m going to break all 4 of them down with tactical examples and tips for how to fix each of them.
What Makes a Winning Strategy?
Live Perfomance
Before I walk through the 4 main mistakes, let’s quickly talk about what a winning strategy actually looks like. Understanding this one thing will help you a ton.
Strategies really only have one job:
to identify high-probability setups and execute them consistently with positive expectancy.
The strategy needs to capture edge and deploy capital efficiently.
To do that, the strategy only has to give you two things:
signal clarity
and
statistical edge
. Signal clarity means you have crystal clear entry and exit rules. Statistical edge means you have confidence the strategy will generate profits over time, validated beyond just curve-fitting.
If you deliver these two things, you win. The million-dollar question is: how do I make sure every strategy I build delivers those two things?
This is where the four strategy mistakes come in. Bad strategies fail because you’re making one or more of these four mistakes. Fix all four, and you’ll be good to go.
