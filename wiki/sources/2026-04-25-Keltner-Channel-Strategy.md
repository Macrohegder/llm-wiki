The Keltner Channel
Trading Strategy (Keltner Bands)
is a lesser-known sibling of the Bollinger Bands, which are widely used and well-known. Although relatively unknown, Keltner Bands are still included in most software packages.
In this post, we present a strategy for stocks.
We get this equity curve on the S&P 500 (SPY) from inception until today:
There are 288 trades, with a win ratio of 77%, a CAGR of 6.3%, and a time spent in the market of 15%. The profit factor is 2.
Trading rules:
The close closes below the lower band. Enter at the close.
Sell when the close is above the “typical price”. Exit at the close.
Optimize by using a period from 2 to 25 days.
We optimize by using different ATRs from 1 to 2.
The best results are clustered around 6-10 days (periods) and an ATR from 1 to 1.5. We backtest using six days and a 1.3 ATR.
