Why Holding a Strategy Is the Real Challenge
You backtest a great system.
It makes money. It’s statistically sound. It’s diversified. You even run a Monte Carlo.
Then you go live...
and after a few weeks of pain, you abandon it.
Not because the math was wrong.
But because your brain was llike:
“This doesn’t feel right!”
Let’s fix that.
1. Optimize for Comfort, Not Just Return
Some people chase performance metrics like:
CAGR
Sharpe Ratio
Net Profit
But the real question is:
Can you actually hold this system during a -12% drawdown?
Here’s a better way to think:
Look at
MAR
: Return ÷ Max Drawdown
Ask yourself: Would I rather make 10% with a 5% drawdown (MAR = 2.0), or 25% with a 20% drawdown (MAR = 1.25)?
Sure, the second one is higher return.
But it’s also more likely to make you panic-quit after a few red days.
Aim for MAR ≥ 1.5 if you want psychological stability.
Here, you can design your own RealTest portfolios with a MAR ratio above 1.25. (in seconds)
Test Your “Quit Scenarios”
Forget the pretty equity curve.
What happens when things go wrong?
Ask yourself:
What if I lose
10% in one month
will I still follow the rules?
What if I have
7 losses in a row
will I trust the system?
What if the system is flat for
6 months
will I start doubting?
If your answer is “No” to any of those...
You don’t need to change the market.
You need to
adjust your system to match your personality
.
That might mean:
Lower sizing
Tighter filters
More diversification
Slower timeframes
Simpler logic
Use Your System Personality Profile
If you hate choppy PnLs, stop forcing yourself into fast mean-reversion.
If you like simplicity and holding for weeks, maybe trend-following or weekly rotation is more your style.
Match your systems to you, not someone else’s X screenshots.
The Real Edge Isn’t Just Math
Here’s what we’ve seen across hundreds of traders:
Systems don’t usually fail.
Humans fail to hold them.
But now you know how to avoid that:
Focus on comfort (not just returns)
Simulate your personal “panic points”
Match your system to your stress profile
Build trust, not just code
If you’ve read all three parts, congrats.
You’re already ahead of 96% of traders chasing shiny metrics instead of stable systems.
“Without Multiple Strategies, We’re Nothing.”
We recently had conversations with several systematic traders including independent quants and even a quant fund managers.
You know what every single one of them said?
“If I had to keep only one thing in my trading process…
I’d keep combining multiple uncorrelated strategies.”
Not execution tools.
Not fancy filters.
Not AI or machine learning.
Just diversification.
It wasn’t even a debate.
Because they’ve probably lived through it the pain of relying on a single system and watching it break at the wrong time.
So, Diversification Is What Makes a System Holdable
We talked about this briefly in Part 1, when we covered regime-specific systems.
But it’s worth repeating and living by:
Trend-following
handles calm bull markets
Long-side mean reversion
thrives in choppy uptrends
Short-side mean reversion
gives edge in falling markets
ETF rotation or volatility strategies
add stability across regimes
Each strategy handles a different type of pain.
Together, they buffer the experience.
So when one struggles… another helps hold the line.
