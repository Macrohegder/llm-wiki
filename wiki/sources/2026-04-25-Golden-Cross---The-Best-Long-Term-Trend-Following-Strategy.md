Why it works
Stocks tend to rise over the long run.
The Golden Cross doesn’t try to predict rallies.
Instead, it gives up perfect entries and exits in exchange for avoiding the worst market declines. You accept occasional whipsaws in order to survive major bear markets.
The real edge
The advantage isn’t higher returns - it’s staying invested long enough for compounding to work.
Many investors don’t fail because of a bad strategy, but because they cannot tolerate deep drawdowns. A trend filter helps solve that psychological challenge.
The Golden Cross is fundamentally defensive.
It reminds us that good strategies don’t need to win every year. They just need to keep you in the game.
