Bitcoin is often dismissed as unpredictable, chaotic, and impossible to value, but for systematic traders, those very characteristics are an opportunity.
Unlike traditional assets, Bitcoin exhibits large and persistent price swings, both up and down, making it particularly well-suited for trend-following and momentum strategies. At least, that is how it has behaved in the past. As the asset matures, its characteristics may change.
What Is A Momentum Bitcoin Strategy?
Rather than trying to forecast where Bitcoin
should
go, momentum strategies take a simpler approach: they follow what the market is already doing.
When prices rise, they tend to keep rising; when they fall, they often continue to fall, a phenomenon well documented across many asset classes, especially stocks. The momentum tends to work for 1-12 months intervals.
In this article, we explore a simple
Bitcoin momentum strategy
built on the idea of capturing major trends while avoiding the worst drawdowns.
This is a strategy we published on our blog many years ago.
