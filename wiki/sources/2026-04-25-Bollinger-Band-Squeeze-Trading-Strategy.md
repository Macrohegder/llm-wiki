Bollinger Band Squeeze Trading Strategy
is a volatility-based approach that aims to capture explosive price moves after periods of market calm.
Financial markets move in cycles, alternating between quiet consolidation and sharp expansion.
The Bollinger Band Squeeze takes advantage of this rhythm by identifying moments when volatility contracts to unusually low levels, signaling that a significant move is likely imminent.
What Is the Bollinger Band Squeeze?
A squeeze occurs when the upper and lower Bollinger Bands narrow significantly around the price, reflecting a period of low volatility.
Bollinger Band Squeeze Trading Strategy
This compression is often described as a “coiled spring”: the longer the market stays compressed, the stronger the potential breakout.
Importantly, the squeeze does not predict direction, only that a large move is coming.
How the Strategy Works
The strategy typically unfolds in three phases:
Contraction:
Volatility declines, and the bands tighten
Breakout:
Price moves decisively above or below the bands
Expansion:
Volatility increases, often leading to a strong trend
Traders look to enter positions when price breaks outside the bands, presumably with confirmation such as rising volume or momentum indicators.
Basic Trading Rules
A common ruleset might look like this:
Identify a squeeze (bands at historically low width)
Enter long on a close above the upper band
Enter short on a close below the lower band
Place a stop-loss near the opposite band
Use a risk-reward ratio (e.g., 2:1) or trail the trade
These rules help systematize what is otherwise a visually simple pattern.
