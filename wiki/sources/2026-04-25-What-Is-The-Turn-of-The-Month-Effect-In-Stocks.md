What is the turn of the month effect in stocks?
The Turn of the Month effect, also known as the Ultimo Effect and the End of Month Effect, is a well-documented market anomaly where stock prices tend to rise during the final days of one month and the first few days of the next.
While many investors focus on the end of the month, research shows the momentum frequently spills over into the first three trading days of the new month.

More importantly, the strategy offers significantly lower drawdowns. For the S&P 500, the maximum drawdown was only 27%, compared to 56% for buy and hold. This effect has also been observed in other markets, including emerging markets, real estate stocks, and even Bitcoin.
Why Does It Happen?
While not proven beyond a reasonable doubt, the effect is likely driven by structural forces. These include:
Automated inflows:
Regular capital contributions from savers and retirement accounts at the start of the month.
Institutional rebalancing:
Fund managers adjusting their portfolios as the month concludes.
Dividend reinvestments:
Large-scale reinvesting of monthly dividends.
Because these structural behaviors are embedded in how financial systems operate, we believe the Turn of the Month strategy is likely to continue performing well in the future.
