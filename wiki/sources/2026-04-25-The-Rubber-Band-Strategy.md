The Rubber Band trading strategy
is a simple mean-reversion approach built on a powerful idea: markets tend to snap back after being stretched too far in one direction. Just like a rubber band, price rarely moves in a straight line forever. Eventually, it reverts toward its average.
The strategy takes a contrarian stance by buying when prices are overly depressed and selling when they become excessively extended.
Traders typically use indicators such as Bollinger Bands or Keltner Channels to identify these extremes and define entry points.
What makes this approach appealing is its simplicity and logic. Instead of chasing momentum, it exploits short-term overreactions and crowd behavior: buying when fear peaks and selling when optimism becomes excessive.
