A recent study titled “
A Century of Profitable Industry Trends
”, authored by Carlo Zarattini and Gary Antonacci, shows that a simple industry-based trend-following system (buy on breakouts, exit on trailing stops, and weight positions by volatility) delivered roughly 18 % annualized returns since 1926 with strong risk-adjusted results (48 different industries).

Results
The industry trend-following strategy delivered an annualized return of 18.2%, almost double the 9.7% achieved by the passive U.S. equity market. Risk-adjusted performance was markedly stronger, with a Sharpe ratio of 1.39 versus 0.63 for the market.
The timing component also provided substantial drawdown protection, cutting the maximum loss by nearly 60% - a peak drawdown of just 33% compared to an 84% collapse in equities. Overall, the strategy exhibits a convex return profile, capturing much of the upside (Upside Beta of 1.16) while sharply reducing exposure during market declines (Downside Beta of 0.31).
A Practical Strategy Using ETFs from 2005
In the final part of their paper, Zarattini and Antonacci move from a century-long historical backtest to a practical, tradable version of the strategy using Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs).
This implementation, spanning January 2005 to March 2024, aimed to test the strategy’s effectiveness in recent markets while accounting for real-world trading costs and practical constraints.
Even after accounting for trading costs of USD 0.0035 per trade, the industry trend-following strategy implemented with ETFs delivered stronger risk-adjusted results than a passive market exposure (SPY):
