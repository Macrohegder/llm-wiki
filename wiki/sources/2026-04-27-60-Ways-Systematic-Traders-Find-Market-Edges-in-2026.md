2. Factor Edges (Academically Proven)
Decades of academic research have identified persistent return premiums "factors" that explain why certain stocks outperform.

3. Information Edges
An information edge means you know something (legally!) before the market prices it in. This is arguably the most powerful edge category but also the hardest to build.
Real-world example:
One thing I've learned is that the best information edges come from combining domain expertise with publicly available data. Here's a great example:
A friend of mine works in the aviation industry and exclusively trades airline and aviation stocks. His secret sauce?

5. Structural Edges
Structural edges come from the way markets are organized rules, mechanics, and participant behavior that create repeatable inefficiencies.
These are often the most persistent edges because they're built into the market's “DNA”.
Here's a structural edge many people don't talk about:
crypto markets have massive wicks
those huge candle tails where price crashes 10–20% and snaps back within minutes.
Why does this happen? Because crypto allows extremely high leverage. Most crypto traders max out their margin 10x, 25x, even 100x. When the market drops, it triggers a
cascade of forced liquidations
. Brokers and exchanges are forced to close all these leveraged positions at market price, which pushes the price even lower. But then once the liquidations are done there are no more sellers, and price bounces right back.
21 Nov 2025:
Bitcoin Flash Crash Triggers Nearly $2B in Liquidations
If you understand this mechanic, you can place limit orders at deep discount levels during high-leverage periods and let the liquidation cascade come to you. Combine this with monitoring liquidation data from tools like
Coinglass
to see where the leverage is stacking up and you have a structural edge that most traders don't even know exists.
6. Volatility Edges
Volatility is one of the most mispriced things in financial markets.
If you understand how to trade it, the edge can be enormous.
Warning:
Volatility edges can blow up spectacularly if you don't manage risk.
The VRP (selling options) can work 85% of the time but the 15% can wipe you out if you're over-leveraged. Always size these strategies conservatively and have a hard stop for maximum loss per trade and per month.
