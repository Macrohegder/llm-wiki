What is an Investment Bank?

An investment bank is known to be a financial intermediary which provides various services relating to financial transactions. These services are underwriting, facilitating mergers and acquisitions (M&A), sales and trading etc. Investment banks are not the ones which receive/accept deposits from the public. They guide the investors and provide capital investments to firms or organizations. Also, they provide investment services to provide benefits to investors.

Investment banks are different from commercial banks in that the commercial banks support large corporations and businesses with several activities such as opening a checking or savings account, borrowing money, getting access to credit facilities etc.

Let us move ahead and find out the role of investment bankers.

What Does an Investment Banker Do?

An investment banker, at the entry-level, usually finds himself/herself wearing many hats as a fresh graduate. People who enjoy challenges and love to find solutions as they go, can aspire to become an investment banker.

In general, investment bankers:

Advise clients on financial decisions

Analyse the performance of the firm on operational as well as financial basis

Coordinate operational process

Train or supervise the junior bankers or team members

Perform valuation of securities

Help clients restructure debt

Now, to be more specific, let us find out the jobs at an investment bank.

Jobs at an Investment Bank

The career of an investment banker advances fairly enough from bottom to top and is quite straightforward and simple to understand. Specific jobs for you from the junior level to the senior level at an investment bank are:

Analyst

Associate

Vice President (VP)

Managing Director (MD)

Analyst

Any fresher in investment banking begins with the role of an analyst and is also known as an investment banking analyst. They are the ones who are responsible for the majority of the tasks which help them gain the required experience in this domain.

Analysts are at the entry level and their main tasks on a daily basis are taking care of the analysis, presentations and administration. Investment analysts are the ones who find their niche and become specialists in a particular chosen field.

For the role of an investment analyst, you will basically require a bachelor’s degree in finance or business administration. Moreover, it is also common to see the freshers or graduates in accounting, statistics, mathematics and economics making it as an analyst. After this position, an investment analyst usually moves up to the position of a senior investment analyst or portfolio manager. The step after this is Chief Investment Officer (CIO). The average base salary after investment analyst position ranges between $1,26,000 and $1,74,000.

The average base salary of an investment analyst is $75,815 according to glassdoor.

Associate

Associates are above analysts and work very closely with them. Associates mainly check the work of analysts and assign the tasks to them. Associates do an in-depth review of the analysts’ tasks and go through models, inputs as well as the outputs to find out if the numbers make sense.

Associates are also directly recruited apart from some analysts having had been promoted. If an associate stays in this position for three and a half years, he/she will be promoted to the Vice President level. But it can also take more than three and a half years for the associate to be promoted in case the requirement at the VP level is not there.

For becoming an investment associate, you need a bachelor’s degree and in some cases a master’s degree also in the same subjects required of an investment analyst. The average base salary for an investment associate in U.S is $72,002 according to glassdoor. For those considering a transition to quantitative finance, understandingquant salarypackages can help evaluate competitive compensation in this field.

Vice Presidents & Managing Directors (VPs & MDs)

The VPs & MDs come under the senior management or the senior bankers’ category. They play a vital role in making sure that the industry landscape is dealt in a detailed manner. They help to anticipate the deals in the investment sector.

The senior bankers reach this stage with a background that ranges from investment banking to corporate executive management. They mainly build relationships with the clients and source deals. Also, they are the ones who find out the capital requirements of the firm as well as the need for strategic discussions.

VPs and MDs are appointed up the ladder only after serving as the investment associate. VPs are appointed after three and a half years of being investment associate whereas, MDs are appointed after about ten years.

The salary range of a VP and MD goes upto $114000.

Qualifications to Get a Job at an Investment Bank

For getting a job at an investment bank, anyone who is passionate about mathematical and analytical setup can aspire to become an investment banker.

Since investment bankers at the entry level are hired from undergraduate programs like B.Com or B.A. from a recognised university, it is not mandatory to have a degree in a special course.

Although, MBA (Finance) or a commerce background is good to kick start your career in investment banking.

Some diploma and certification courses as the background for investment banking is appreciated in the following subjects:

Financial control

Financial analysis

Equity research

Portfolio management and security analysis

Banking and finance

CFA or Chartered Financial Analyst

Out of all the above mentioned subjects, CFA is the exclusive qualification for being able to grab a senior position in the top investment banking firms.
