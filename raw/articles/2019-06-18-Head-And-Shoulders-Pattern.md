Anatomy of Head and Shoulders pattern

Initially, a peak is formed, like any other in the rising trend,

A second peak higher than the first is formed and finally,

A third peak is formed lower than the second peak.

The role of Volume

In the first peak, the volume is high, as usually corresponds with a rising trend.

In the second peak, the head, the volume is usually lower than in the first, this is a clear indicator that the new peak is not supported by many purchases, at least not as many as in the first peak and finally,

In the third peak, the volume is lower than in the second peak, again indicating that neither the price has been able to overcome the previous peak, nor the volume has been strong enough for the price to continue rising.

In the breakout, the volume usually grows again although it is not a necessary requirement but a good signal.
