### THE BIG PICTURE THIS WEEK


Asset Managers are long bonds at 3-year extremes AND long the S&P at the 95th percentile.

Hedge funds are still stampeded into the soy complex.

Copper producers are hedging like the sky is falling.

And everyone (hedge funds, real money, and retail) is simultaneously piled long the Australian Dollar.

Something gives.


### COMMODITIES



#### COPPER: The Tell


Producers at the1st percentile. Not a typo.

The people who mine, smelt, and physically trade copper are more aggressively short-hedged than atanypoint in the last three years.

Hedge funds are still net long at the **87th percentile**. They haven’t gotten the memo.

Copper is the global growth barometer. When physical-market participants hedge this aggressively, they’re pricing oversupply, demand collapse, or both and the signal shows up weeks before industrial commodities break down.

Now sit with the contradiction: Asset Managers are buying stocks like a soft landing is locked in. Copper commercials are battening down the hatches. One of these groups is (probably) wrong.

Watch: Any decline in Managed Money next week. The exit on this one is narrow.


#### THE SOY COMPLEX: Still Stampeding


Last week Soybean Oil was the crowded long.

This week the stampede spread.

-Soybean Oil: MM 99.6th percentile, PM 3.5th. Over 3 months, MM added +201,000 net long.

-Soybean Meal: MM 95.8th, PM 0.4th. Over 3 months, MM +152,000. Crowded Long flag.

-Soybeans: MM 91.5th, PM 11.9th, but here’s the detail: MM *reduced* their long by -27,000 contracts over the last month.


#### NATURAL GAS: The Crowded Short Nobody’s Talking About


Hedge funds at the5th percentile. Net short 114,000 contracts, and theyaddedanother -69,000 of net short exposure in the last month.

Producers at the 90th comfortably unhedged.

Crowded shorts don’t time themselves, but when they resolve, short-covering rallies in nat gas have historically been violent. Keep on radar.


### FINANCIAL FUTURES



#### TREASURIES: Conviction Held


10Y: Asset Managers at the 96th percentile. +113,000 contracts added in the last month. They did not take profits.

2Y: Asset Managers at the 98th percentile. +133,000 added in the last month. When real money loads the short end this aggressively, they’re pricing an imminent rate-cut cycle, a flight-to-quality event, or both.

The defensive bond trade didn’t unwind. It doubled down.


#### US EQUITIES: The Pivot


Here’s where the story breaks.

S&P 500:Asset Managers at the95th percentile. Net long1,011,000 contracts. +127,000 added in the last month. Last week AM was not extended in equities at all. This week they’re at a historical extreme.

Nasdaq 100:AM at the74th percentile. They’re buying tech back too.

Russell 2000:AM at the35th percentile. They’re not buying small caps.

Asset Managers are expressing something like “stocks up, but quality only” the indexes that benefit from falling rates and institutional mandate flows, while leaving the high-beta, high-domestic-sensitivity corner alone.

It’s the most elegant way to be simultaneously long equities and positioned for rate cuts. But it’s still a contradiction.

You can’t have aggressive rate cuts AND an earnings cycle that justifies the S&P at these levels.

One thesis is load-bearing. The other is a hedge. And the Russell, sitting there absent, is telling you which side they actually believe.


#### AUSTRALIAN DOLLAR: Crowded Long, Everyone Edition


- Leveraged Money at the 96th percentile

- Asset Managers at the 98th

- Small Specs at the 98th

Hedge funds, real money, and retail, all three groups near their 3-year max.

Every natural long has bought. Any selling has to be absorbed by dealers who are structurally short at these levels. The bid evaporates on the first catalyst.

Not a directional call. A fragility call.


### CROSS-MARKET THEME: The All-Clear That Isn’t


What Asset Managers just did:

- Kept maximum long bonds.

- Aggressively bought S&P 500 (95th percentile).

- Bought Nasdaq back.

- Left small caps alone.

- Stayed short CHF (no safe-haven hedge).

That’s the positioning of an institution betting on rate cuts and an equity melt-upanda soft landingandno material risk event. The “all-clear” trade.

Except:

- Copper producers are at the 1st percentile.

- The soy complex is stampeded, with Soybeans already starting to roll.

- Australian Dollar has hit universal crowding.

- Natural Gas has hedge funds at a 3-year max short.

Copper hasn’t crashed yet.

AUD hasn’t unwound yet.

Soy hasn’t broken yet.

But they’re all loaded with the same fragility: positions extended enough that a small catalyst produces an outsized reaction (or not :))

Last week’s defensive rotation didn’t resolve.

It got more contradictory.

Institutions are now positioned for every scenario at once which is another way of saying they’re hedged against none.

That’s not where I’d want to be standing when one of these finally snaps.
