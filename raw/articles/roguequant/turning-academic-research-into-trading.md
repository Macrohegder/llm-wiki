One of my go-to sources for trading ideas has always been academic papers.

First off, because someone's already poured dozens of hours into a specific topic. They've already done the heavy lifting on the research side.

Secondly, they'vetheoreticallyvalidated the idea using at least one statistical tool.

I saytheoreticallybecause, let's be honest, not all papers are created equal.

Some follow rigorous development and backtesting standards.

Others... not so much.

But besides that, it’s a solid starting point, way better than just a hunch.

And third (and most importantly), absorbing the core ideas from a paper feels like adding another piece to the puzzle. But this isn't justanypiece.

It's awild card.

The kind that fitsanypuzzle you’re building.
