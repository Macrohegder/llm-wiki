## Equities



### The Russell 2000: Real Money Defected Quietly


Asset Managers at the 15th percentile, meaning their current net long position in the Russell is smaller than it has been in 85% of weekly readings over the last three years.

A week ago the same metric was at the 35th. A 20-point percentile drop in one week,

Hedge funds (Leveraged Money) are sitting net short at the30th percentile. Dealers on the other side at the74th, absorbing.

Non-Reportables in the E-mini DJIA are at the98th percentilewhich is historic long extreme.

The composition of the equity tape just bifurcated: real money owns the top (S&P, Nasdaq), retail owns it below (Dow, dispersed names), and both have walked away from the small-cap story.

Small caps were supposed to be the confirmation trade for “rate cuts are coming, the economy is fine, domestic earnings will catch up.” Asset Managers are not positioned for that anymore.


### S&P 500 + Nasdaq: Conviction Held, Just Concentrated


S&P 500:AM at the91st percentile. Trimmed one notch from 95th, but still+146,000 net longs added over four weeks. They are not taking profits. They are still buying.

Nasdaq 100:AM at the81st percentile,upfrom 74th. More exposure rebuilt aggressively: another +33,000 over the last month.

The equity bet is now explicitly:large-cap, mega-tech, rate-cut sensitive, institutionally-mandated. Not “stocks.” Just the names the mandate has to hold.


## The Curve: A Clean Steepener, Maximum Conviction


The bond book split hard this week.

2-Year:AM at the96th percentile. Still maxed long. Pricing cuts, aggressively.

10-Year:AM at the89th percentile. Slight trim from 96th took some profit, still heavy.

30-Year:AM at the13th percentile. Net long, but at thelow endof a 3-year range.

And on the other side:Leveraged Money at the 98th percentile short… hedge funds at a 3-year maximum short in the long bond.

Read that as a sentence: institutions are long the front, leaning off the middle, andactively short the long end. That is the clean steepener trade.

They want the front-end rally that comes with cuts, but they don’t want the duration risk on the 30-year.
