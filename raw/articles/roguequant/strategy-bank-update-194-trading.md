## Why Most Academic Strategies Don’t Replicate (And Why That’s Actually Good)


After trying to code hundreds of strategies exactly as published, here’s what happened:

Only 8% replicated perfectly.

The other 92%?

Something was always different:

- Data they used isn’t publicly available- Forward-looking bias they didn’t catch- Transaction costs weren’t properly modeled- They forgot to mention key filters in the paper- The edge worked in 2005-2015 but stopped working- “Survivorship bias-free data” means their proprietary dataset

Most traders see this as failure/frustration…

I see it as the entire point.

Because when you can’t replicate something, you’re forced to go deeper and understand the nuances.

And that’s where the insights live.


## The Real Value: Insight Generation, Not Strategy Replication


Here’s my actual workflow now:

Step 1:Read paper, understand the core concept

Step 2:Try to replicate exactly (this almost always fails)

Step 3:Ask: “What insight can I extract from this?”

Step 4:Test variations using that insight

Step 5:Keep what works, document what doesn’t

With this workflow I can test dozens of strategies in a week.

Even more with the help of LLMs.
