How the Backtest Was Conducted
The goal of the backtest was to move beyond subjective chart reading and into rule-based signal generation. The methodology followed these specific parameters:
Universe Selection:
We used the SPY (SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust) to represent a liquid, realistic trading environment.
Historical Data:
The study utilized daily OHLCV (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) data from 1993-02-01 through the present.
Pattern Detection:
Formations were identified using proprietary code rather than visual inspection. We have the code and rules in plain English.
Trade Execution:
Patterns were detected at the close of day
t
, with an entry at that same close.
Holding Periods:
We tested various short-term horizons, including 1, 2, 3, 5, and up to 20 trading days.
Ranking Criteria:
Patterns were ranked based on their Profit Factor (Gross Profit ÷ Gross Loss), annualized return, and trade frequency.
Candlestick Patterns That Actually Work: The 10 Best
The following patterns represent the top performers in our SPY backtest, ranked from ten down to one: 10. Bearish Separating Lines 9. Bullish Marubozu 8. Long-Legged Doji 7. Neutral Doji 6. Bullish Harami 5. Three Inside Up 4. Bullish Piercing Line 3. Dark Cloud Cover 2. Three Outside Down
The winner was:
Bearish Engulfing
A significant takeaway from this data is that many patterns with “bearish” names, such as the Bearish Engulfing, actually function as bullish mean-reversion signals in the stock market. This is because stocks tend to revert to the mean more reliably than other assets, such as forex or crypto.
The Combined Candlestick Trading Strategy
The most robust results were not found in individual signals but by combining the five best patterns into a single rule-based strategy.
To optimize performance, we modified the exit rule: rather than a fixed holding period, the strategy exits the position when the price closes above the previous day’s high. For the combined test, we included a small transaction cost of 0.03% per trade to maintain realism.
