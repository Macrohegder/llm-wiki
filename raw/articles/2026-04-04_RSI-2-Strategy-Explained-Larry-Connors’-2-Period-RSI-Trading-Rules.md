RSI 2 Strategy Explained: Larry Connors’ 2-Period RSI Trading Rules is one of the simplest and most effective mean reversion strategies ever published.
Developed by Larry Connors, the RSI 2 strategy is a premier mean reversion approach designed to exploit short-term price reversals. Unlike traditional momentum strategies that chase breakouts, this system focuses on buying during short-term panic when the market is oversold and selling during periods of euphoria.
The Mechanism: Why Use a 2-Period RSI?
The standard Relative Strength Index (RSI) typically uses a 14-period lookback, which provides a smoothed view of the market.
However, the RSI 2 compresses this calculation to just two bars, creating an indicator of extreme sensitivity. This reactivity allows the strategy to capture fast-moving reversals; for instance, two strong down days can push the RSI 2 near zero, signaling a potential buying opportunity.
Strategy Rules and Backtest Results
To validate the effectiveness of this approach, we look at a specific backtest for SPY (the ETF tracking the S&P 500) covering the period from 1993 to the present.
The rules for this setup are straightforward:
Buy Signal:
Enter at the close when the
RSI 2 drops below 10
.
Sell Signal:
Exit the position when the
RSI 2 crosses above 80
.
RSI 2 Strategy Explained
The performance metrics for this simple systematic approach are as follows:
Average Gain per Trade:
0.9%
Annual Returns (CAGR):
9%
Maximum Drawdown:
34%
Time Invested:
28%
Key Takeaway
The most striking insight from these results is the efficiency of the strategy:
It generates a 9% annual return while only being invested 28% of the time. This allows traders to achieve significant market participation while keeping capital liquid for other opportunities the majority of the time.
However, the strategy does experience a 34% maximum drawdown during volatile periods, which is probably too high for most traders to stomach. It’s easy to abandon a strategy with such drawdowns.
