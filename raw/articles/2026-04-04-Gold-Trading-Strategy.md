Implementing a gold trading strategy doesn’t need to be complex. In this article, we show you an example of how you can do it yourself as a retail trader.
Today’s strategy was originally developed by Allocate Smartly, and we recommend checking out their website (they have many interesting articles).
Gold is commonly seen as a safe-haven asset, yet its price doesn’t always move upward consistently. A straightforward trend-following (momentum) strategy can help identify the right times to invest - and when to stay out.
Gold Trading Strategy - Rules
The strategy uses two 12-month total return signals:
One for gold (GLD) and one for 10-year U.S. Treasuries (IEF).
You go long gold only when both returns are positive at month-end.
If either return is negative, you remain in cash until the next signal.
This dual-momentum filter helps avoid many weak periods. It’s intentionally simple -fewer parameters mean less risk of overfitting.
How To Implement A Gold Trend-Following Strategy
(Source images: Allocate Smartly website.)
Over the past 50 years, this approach has typically cut exposure during down periods and outperformed a simple gold-only momentum strategy. That said, it has been less effective during the strong bull market of the past two decades, since GLD was launched.
In backtests, this rule generated an annualized return of around 6% for GLD, compared with roughly 10% for a simple buy-and-hold approach (actual results vary depending on the period tested and cash returns).
The primary advantage is avoiding prolonged stretches of poor gold performance. Since gold can underperform for long periods and cash returns can vary, this strategy works best as part of a diversified portfolio.
For instance, you might combine it with a few other trend- or momentum-based strategies alongside the gold trend-following approach.
