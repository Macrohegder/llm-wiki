How to trade stocks in play?
Today, we show you one strategy that we know day traders use, but perhaps not exactly the trading rules explained below.
The “Stocks in Play” strategy is built around the concept that day traders earn the biggest edge when they focus only on stocks with unusually high trading activity right at the open, so-called
stocks in play
. These are names with elevated volume and volatility due to news or catalysts, making them prime candidates for strong intraday moves.
A very good and detailed research paper by Carlo Zarattini, Andrea Barbon, and Andrew Aziz called
A Profitable Day Trading Strategy For The U.S. Equity Market
offers a very detailed strategy that we have chosen to call the "stocks in play trading strategy”. To make it work, you have to tweak it, though. There is no free lunch!
Below, we explain what the authors did:
The Trading System — Opening Range Breakout (ORB)
At its heart, the strategy uses a classic
Opening Range Breakout (ORB)
system:
• Define the high/low of the first 5-minute bar.
• Go long if the price breaks above the high when the initial range is bullish; short if it breaks below the low when bearish.
• Use an ATR-based stop loss and exit all positions by the end of the day.
Why Most ORB Results Are Weak on Their Own
Applied broadly to all liquid stocks (price > $5, avg volume > 1M, ATR > $0.50), the 5-minute ORB produced only a 29% total return over 7 years, thus underperforming a passive S&P 500 buy-and-hold.
However, it can be improved, according to the authors:
The Breakthrough: Focus on Stocks in Play
The strategy becomes powerful when two filters are added:
Stocks in play strategy - ranked on relative volume improves the strategy
Only trade stocks whose
first-5-minute Relative Volume (RV) exceeds 100%
, indicating unusually high early activity.
Trade only the
top 20 stocks by RV each day
.
With these constraints, performance transforms dramatically:
• Total return jumps to 1,637%
• Annualized return exceeds 40%
• Sharpe ratio improves to 2.8
• Returns are largely uncorrelated to the market.
How To Trade Stocks In Play (Strategy)
Best Time Frame: 5 Minutes
The best time frame is 5 minutes:
Stocks in play trading strategy - a 5-minute lookback period is best
Shorter opening range definitions outperform longer ones. The 5-minute ORB captures the strongest intraday trends and delivers superior returns versus 15, 30, or 60-minute ranges.
That said, you need to backtest yourself. We know for sure that several traders use trading rules (successfully) that are somewhat similar to the ones in this research article. We recommend that you backtest yourself to find the best way
how to trade stocks in play
.
