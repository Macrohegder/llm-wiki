The Reality Behind the Numbers
A 52% win rate does not look impressive on paper.
There is no “high accuracy” narrative here. No forecasting edge. No macro calls.
Yet the portfolio compounds at ~27% annually.
That is the first thing most people misunderstand about systematic trading.
The edge is small.
It only becomes meaningful when it is repeated hundreds of times, across different markets, under controlled risk.
The outcome is not driven by any single trade.
It is driven by process.
The Hard Lesson: Risk Management
The most important moment in this journey was not a winning streak.
It was a drawdown.
On March 20th, 2025, the portfolio hit a maximum drawdown of −42%.
This was not caused by the strategies “stopping working.”
The edge was still there. Most of my strategies was “supposed” to lose money in that environment.
The problem was position sizing.
At the time, I was using static position sizes. The same fixed contract size, regardless of market conditions.
But volatility had increased.
That meant:
Larger price swings
Higher risk per trade
Greater exposure during losing streaks
When a cluster of losing trades hit, positions were simply too large relative to the actual risk in the market.
Losses were amplified.
This is the part that is easy to miss:
The strategy did not fail.
The risk management did.
What Changed
After that drawdown, I made a structural shift.
I moved away from static sizing and introduced:
Volatility-adjusted position sizing (ATR-based)
Lower risk per trade
Greater focus on portfolio-level risk
Position size now scales with market conditions.
Higher volatility → smaller positions
Lower volatility → larger positions

This keeps risk more stable over time.
The result is not smoother returns.
The result is survivability.
I like to think about that huge drawdown as the cost of learning this important lesson, it was a pretty expensive course basically. The most important thing in a situation like this, is for a trader to quickly realize what the loss is coming from and if it’s in line with historical losses.
The conviction to let robust strategies run when in drawdown is one of the main components of a traders long-term success.
Systematic trading is not just about finding an edge.
It is about staying in the game long enough for that edge to compound.
How Small Edges Compound
One of the most counterintuitive aspects of systematic trading is how small the edge can be.
Win rate: 52%
Average holding time: ~2–3 days
Lately this win rate has been increased as the amount of failed/cancelled strategies has decreased overtime. As a systematic trader there will always be times when you doubt a strategy and cancel it or realize that the strategy you are trading is actually losing its edge and you have to remove it, these are moments that you can’t avoid.
The edge comes from identifying small, repeatable inefficiencies and executing them consistently.
Over time:
Small gains accumulate
Losses are contained
The distribution of outcomes stabilizes
Compounding does the rest.
Diversification and position sizing do most of the heavy lifting.
Not prediction.
Where the Profits Actually Come From
Another observation becomes clear after enough trades.
Profits are concentrated.
Share of total gains from my most traded markets:
US Tech 100: ~36%
Germany 40: ~31%
Gold: ~28%
A small number of markets generate the majority of returns.
This highlights an important reality:
You do not need to trade everything.
You need to identify a few markets where your edge consistently appears.
More markets do not automatically mean better performance.
Better edges do.

Final Thought
Systematic trading is a long-term game.
Most people quit during the drawdowns. They change strategies at the worst possible moment, right when the edge is about to reassert itself.
The traders who compound over years are not the ones with the best strategies.
They are the ones who stayed disciplined long enough for the math to work in their favor.
469 trades in, that is the clearest lesson this portfolio has taught me.
The edge was never the hard part.
Executing it consistently through losing streaks, through doubt, through a −42% drawdown, that is where most people fail.
And that is exactly where the real returns come from.
