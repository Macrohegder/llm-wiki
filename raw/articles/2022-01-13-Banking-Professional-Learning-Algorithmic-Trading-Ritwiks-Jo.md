## 原文

15 years in Banking to now Algo Trading | Ritwik's journey

Ritwik has 15+ years of experience in the banking industry and is skilled in Banking, Foreign Exchange (FX), Derivatives, Treasury, Equity & Debt Financing.

He holds a BTech in IT, a Post Graduate in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) from the University of Texas. He is an expert treasury professional (Certified Treasury Professional) with a CAIIB, from the Indian Institute of Banking and Finance (IIBF).

A proud EPAT alumnus, we connected with him to learn of his journey into Algorithmic trading and Quantitative trading.

Hi Ritwik, tell us about yourself!

Hi, I’mRitwik Guha Thakurta. I am serving as Head of Treasury in Nepal SBI Bank (a joint venture between India and Nepal government). It is the biggest (in terms of operation) and the only listed subsidiary of SBI (The State Bank of India) outside India.

Football is my passion. As a kid, I spent a lot of time on the field. I also like to read books on various subjects. I especially read a lot about upcoming technologies as I like keeping myself updated there.

I worked in Mphasis for three years as a software engineer after I completed BTech in 2006 in Information Technology. It was there that I discovered my deep interest in the finance field, thanks to the time I spent working on financial products and clients so I switched my career to join SBI as a Probationary Officer in 2009.

Being exposed to both finance and technology, I believe that the future of finance will be fueled by technology and that’s what led me to pursue a PG in AI & ML.

What has your journey into the world of algorithmic trading from IT been like?

I got placed at Mphasis through campus. It was a dream to land in a tech company back then due to the tech boom at that time. There I was in BFSI vertical and looked after Credit Card operations of a multinational bank in Japan.

This is what got me interested in the financial field and I moved to the field of finance by joining SBI. My target was always being in Treasury as I wanted to be a trader.

I got my first Treasury front office assignment in 2013. I was thrilled to enter the dealing room, but I couldn't understand a single word. Over the years, I deep-dived into it, learnt a lot and moved my way up.

I first heard about algorithmic trading in 2015 and since then, I was curious about it.

After completing my PG, I wanted to enhance my knowledge and expertise in algorithmic trading further. I also wanted some specific domain skills and guidance; therefore I enrolled myself for EPAT at QuantInsti.

Was getting into Algo Trading challenging for you with just the initial knowledge of finance, statistics and programming?

Yes. Algo Trading is challenging. However, knowledge and skills attained from my PG and prior experience helped me.

If one doesn’t have experience with programming or finance or statistics, might take some time to build expertise but you will be able to achieve your goal.

The best part of this journey:

I have met Fantastic Faculties

Learning at EPAT provided me with great fulfilment

Concept-wise everything is crystal clear

Implementation wise - believe me, from my experience when I say there is a vast difference when an academician teaches and a practitioner teaches.

It was a rewarding experience at EPAT

Before joining EPAT, I was not sure how to do algorithmic trading at all.

I wondered:Will I be able to learn algo trading?After doing EPAT, will I be able to convince myself to start my trading desk?

But after completing the programme I’m confident enough to start my venture with the support of QuantInsti. This may take some time, but ultimately, I will do it.

What would be your message to the aspiring algo traders out there?

Although your trading will be automated, it will be based on your strategy and without emotions mixed in it. You don't have to sit through the entire day in front of the screen. That's the advantage of algo trading.

Clear fundamentals -From my experience, my first advice is - fundamentals should be very strong. Then only jump into trading. You implement either fundamental, technical or sentimental strategies, so get a fair idea about them. Algo trading will facilitate to implementation of your strategy.

Flexibility. Learning. Automation.

Today, Tech and finance are not separate - that is why fintech is the new buzzword.

Unlike manual trading, Algo trading gives you the flexibility of keeping different strategies (fundamental, quantitative or quantamental) in one place. That is the power of algo trading.
