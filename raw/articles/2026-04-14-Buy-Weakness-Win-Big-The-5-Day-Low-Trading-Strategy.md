Buy Weakness, Win Big: The 5-Day Low Trading Strategy is built on a simple but uncomfortable idea: you make money by buying what everyone else is eager to sell.
Most traders do the opposite. They chase strength. They buy breakouts. They feel safe when prices are rising (perhaps FOMO is at play).
But markets don’t reward comfort. They reward discipline and, often, a willingness to act when things look temporarily ugly.
The 5-day low setup flips the script. Instead of chasing momentum, you lean into short-term weakness. Not blindly, but systematically.
And that distinction is everything.
Because without rules, “buy the dip” quickly turns into “catch a falling knife.”
Buy Weakness, Win Big: The 5-Day Low Trading Strategy
At its core, this is a classic mean reversion idea.
Markets tend to overshoot in the short term. When prices fall for several days in a row, they often push too far, too fast. That creates short-term imbalances, and those imbalances tend to revert.
Quantified strategies work precisely because they remove judgment and force you to act on repeatable patterns instead of emotions.
The 5-day low is one such pattern.
It identifies short-term pessimism  and bets on a snapback. Such snapbacks are more predictable in the “heavier” stock indices.
The strategy (plain English)
The logic is simple:
You look for the S&P 500 to trade at a 5-day low, meaning today’s price is the lowest compared to the last five trading days.
